A Closer Look at AI's Mistakes

Hypothesized Error Distribution

We anticipate that errors in complex, multi-step reasoning will be the most common failure point for the AI, rather than simple factual recall.

Sample Error Analysis

Click on an error type to see a representative example of a question where the AI might falter.

Example (Physics):

"A solid sphere rolls without slipping down an incline of 30°. Find its acceleration."

Potential AI Flaw:

The AI might correctly identify the formulas for linear and rotational motion but fail to correctly synthesize them by linking linear acceleration (a) and angular acceleration (α) through the no-slip condition (a = αR), leading to an incorrect final answer.

Example (Chemistry):

"Calculate the pH of a 0.05 M solution of H₂SO₄."

Potential AI Flaw:

The AI might correctly recognize that H₂SO₄ is a strong acid but treat it as monoprotic, calculating pH based only on the initial H⁺ concentration and ignoring the second dissociation step, resulting in a simple but critical calculation mistake.

Example (Math):

"Determine if the function f(x) = |x-2| is differentiable at x=2."

Potential AI Flaw:

While the AI knows the rules of differentiation, it might fail to grasp the geometric concept that a sharp corner (a 'cusp') in a graph means the derivative is undefined at that point. It might try to apply power rules incorrectly instead of checking the limit definition of a derivative from the left and right.
